Private Enterprise Public Safety Act of 2021 (PEPSA):

  • Small Tax on private security companies - Create revenue for oversight and facility maintenance.
  • Tax break for public safety duty - less tax for companies who accept the responsibility of non law enforcement public first responder duties such as medical aid on city sidewalks and in city streets, traffic management at accident scenes not yet controlled by a governmental emergency service, violent incident and active shooter response. (Duty to break up a fight in the street or stop an active shooter, anywhere, anytime)
  • Establishes violet or purple lights as security specific traffic advisory lighting. Establishes legal requirement of right of way for flashing purple lights, Allows for sirens w/ state registration for the purpose of burglar alarm response, Internal backup requests and public safety render of aid. Defines *no legal right to conduct a traffic stop* in public. Lights are for traffic advisory, security guard identification and Priority response for burglar alarm response, etc.
  • Increase training requirements to include all tier training plus Emergency Vehicle driver training for purple lights.
  • Establish alternative means of information exchange with law enforcement, freeing up 911 and providing better inter-agency information. This will allow private security companies to contribute in the search for stolen property and missing persons, as well as allow private security companies to submit low level service requests to law enforcement without the need to contact 911.
  • The privatization option: The body of citizens shall, by measure of popular vote, determine whether the current municipal police force employed by the institutional governmental body is providing adequate service at a more cost effective value than could be provided by a private corporation. Utilizing the same guidelines as set forth by the privatization of our prison systems, a city shall determine whether the municipal force shall remain, or be replaced by a private entity such as a private security company. Regardless of the outcome of the decision, the vote to retain or replace entirely the organization responsible for law enforcement in our community shall occur every two(2) years.
  • In the event an enforcement entity, regardless of the ownership of the entity assets, shall vacate taxpayer owner law enforcement structures and equipment and execute a peaceful and efficient transfer or power.
